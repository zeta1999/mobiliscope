Indicators

People can be characterized in the Mobiliscope according to their demographic and social profile and their residential area, but also according to the nature of their activity and the travel mode they used.

Demographic profile

  • Sex: Female and Male.
  • Age divided into four groups: 16-24 years; 25-34 years; 35-64 years; 65 and more.

Social profile

  • Socioprofessional status. We merged respondents’ socioprofessional data in five groups of individual socioprofessional status: inactive (unemployed long term, housework); low (workers); middle-low (employees); middle-high (intermediary professionals, craftsmen, merchants, employers of more than 10 employees and farm operators); and high (managers and intellectual professionals). We also computed household socioprofessional status (also divided in five groups) corresponding to the lowest socioprofessional category of the adults in the household.
  • Educational status . We merged respondents’ achieved level of education in four groups of individual educational status: low (middle school or less); middle-low (high school without Baccalauréat); middle-high (up to two years after Baccalauréat); and high (three years or more after Baccalauréat). We also computed household educational status (also divided in four groups) corresponding to the lowest level of education of the adults in the household.
  • Household income. Only available in Paris region, total monthly household income has been divided by the number of adults and children in the household to get household income per consumption units (CU). We distinguished four groups: low (< 1084€/CU); middle-low (1084-1806€/CU); middle-high (1806-2890€/CU); high (>2890€/CU). Income intervals have been defined according to median household income in the Paris region (equal to 1806€/CU in 2010). The first threshold (1084€/CU) or 'poverty threshold’ corresponds to 60 % of median household income; the second one (1806€/CU) corresponds to the median household income; the third one (2890€/CU) correspond to 160 % of median household income.
  • We merged respondents' occupational status in five groups of occupational status: Inactive; Retired; Unemployed; Student; Active.

Residential area

  • Districts of residence have been divided into 3 groups : Inner city ; Urban area ; Suburban/peripheral area. 'Inner city' refers to central (administrative) city in charge of the origin-destination survey. 'Urban area' refers to districts which are totally (or mainly) included in large, middle or small urban clusters as defined in the 2010 'Zoning into Urban Areas - ZAU' (French National Institute of Statistics and Economic Studies). Remaining districts are labbeled as belonging to 'suburban/peripheral area'.
  • Departments of residence. Only in the Paris region, respondents' departments of residence were divided into five groups: Paris; Seine-Saint-Denis; Hauts-de-Seine; Val-de-Marne; Greater Paris (Essonne + Seine-et-Marne + Yvelines + Val-d'Oise).

Activity/Travel mode

In contrast with indicators related to individual/household characteristics, indicators related to activity and travel mode may change for one respondent throughout the day.

  • Activity. We distinguished five main groups of activity: At home; At work; Studying; Shopping; Leisure (recreational, cultural or sporting activities, family and personal visits). We have excluded 'unspecified activity' and 'activity related to escorting or chauffeuring'. The sample of locations related to these activities was too small and it does not make sense to gather them with some other activities.
  • Travel mode. We distinguished three main travel modes: soft mobility (walking, cycling ...); private motor vehicle (two-wheeled motor vehicle, company vehicle, taxi etc.); public transportation.
